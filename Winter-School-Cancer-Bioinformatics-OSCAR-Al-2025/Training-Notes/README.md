# Winter School: Tech Fusion in Cancer Bioinformatics and Optical Sensors Diagnostics

**German University in Cairo (GUC), 20–22 December 2025**

## Overview

This folder contains personal training notes from the **Winter School: Tech Fusion in Cancer Bioinformatics and Optical Sensors Diagnostics**, held at the **German University in Cairo (20–22 December 2025)** and organized within the **GARN-CLS** and **OSCAR-AI** collaboration, in partnership with **Universität Rostock**, **Universitätsmedizin Rostock (UMR)**, **EG-CompBio**, and the **ARAtronics Research Center**.

The program brought together researchers from **Egypt, Germany, Tunisia, and Lebanon**, exploring interdisciplinary approaches that integrate **bioinformatics, artificial intelligence, biomedical engineering, and optical sensing technologies** for applications in **cancer research, precision medicine, and next-generation healthcare systems**.

The training covered:

* Precision oncology and genomic medicine
* Integration of **bioinformatics, artificial intelligence, and biomedical engineering**
* Optical biosensors and lab-on-chip technologies for diagnostics
* Computational drug discovery and AI-driven drug development
* Multi-omics analysis in cancer research
* **Single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) analysis workflows**
* Spatial transcriptomics and tumor microenvironment analysis
* Emerging technologies including **virtual reality, digital twins, and healthcare AI systems**

These notes document the **scientific concepts, technologies, and analytical approaches** explored during the three-day winter school.

Example interactive single-cell visualization platforms used during the first day of the Single-Cell workshop:

* **CELLxGENE** — interactive web-based platform for visualizing and exploring single-cell RNA sequencing datasets.
* **bxGenomics** — platform for interactive exploration and visualization of single-cell genomics data.

### Day 2 — Multi-Omics Analysis & Computational Drug Discovery

* Understanding **multi-omics integration in complex disease research**
* Knowledge of **spatial transcriptomics and tumor microenvironment analysis**
* Familiarity with **computational drug discovery pipelines**
* Exposure to **AI-driven target identification and drug development strategies**
* Awareness of national bioinformatics initiatives such as **BioNetMasr**
* Practical experience in **single-cell RNA-seq data analysis using R and Seurat**
 
Key tools and frameworks used in the second day of the Single-Cell workshop:  

* **R programming** — statistical programming language widely used for bioinformatics and computational biology analyses.
* **Seurat** — R package for **single-cell RNA-seq data analysis**, including clustering, visualization, and cell-type identification.
* **Harmony** — algorithm used for **batch effect correction and integration of multiple single-cell datasets**.
* **DESeq2** — statistical package for **differential gene expression analysis in RNA sequencing data**.
* **SingleCellExperiment** — data structure framework in R designed for **storing and managing single-cell genomics datasets**.
* **ggplot2** — visualization package in R used to create **high-quality scientific plots and data visualizations**.
* **patchwork** — R package that allows **combining multiple ggplot2 plots into a single figure layout**.
---

### Day 3 — AI Technologies & Emerging Biomedical Systems

* Understanding emerging **AI-driven healthcare technologies**
* Knowledge of **virtual reality and digital twin systems in biomedical research**
* Awareness of **AI-based remote health monitoring and sensing technologies**
* Exposure to **metabolomics analytical techniques for biomarker discovery**
* Understanding applications of **robotics in laboratory automation**
* Experience applying **computational analysis to single-cell transcriptomics datasets**  

Practical tools used in the third day of the Single-Cell workshop project included: 

* **R** — programming environment used for statistical computing and bioinformatics workflows.
* **Seurat** — framework for **single-cell RNA-seq analysis including clustering, visualization, and marker detection**.
* **SeuratDisk** — package used to **convert and manage Seurat objects across different data formats**.
* **tidyverse** — collection of R packages for **data manipulation, transformation, and visualization**.
* **SCTransform** — normalization method used in Seurat for **variance stabilization and improved single-cell data integration**.
* **PCA** — dimensionality reduction technique used to **identify major sources of variation in gene expression data**.
* **UMAP** — visualization algorithm used to **project high-dimensional single-cell data into two-dimensional space** for cluster visualization.
