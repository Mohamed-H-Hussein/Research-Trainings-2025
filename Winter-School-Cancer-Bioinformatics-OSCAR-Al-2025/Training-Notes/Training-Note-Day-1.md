## Session: Personalized Medicine in Germany — Introducing Complex Molecular Signatures in Patient Care

**speaker:** PD. Dr. Hugo Escobar   
Rostock University Medical Center (UMR), Germany   
**Type:** Lecture 

---

### 1. Global Cancer Burden

Cancer remains one of the leading causes of mortality worldwide.

Key statistics presented:

* **~19.3 million new cancer cases annually worldwide**
* **~10 million cancer-related deaths per year**

Germany statistics:

* **~497,900 new cases per year**
* **~230,000 cancer-related deaths annually**

These numbers highlight the urgent need for **advanced diagnostic strategies and personalized treatment approaches**.

---

### 2. Comprehensive Cancer Centers (CCC)

Germany has developed a **three-tier cancer care structure** to improve clinical outcomes:

#### 1. Oncology Centers of Excellence (CCC)

Characteristics:

* Advanced patient care
* Translational research
* Clinical trials
* Molecular tumor boards
* International collaboration

These centers integrate **research, clinical practice, and education**.

---

#### 2. Oncology Centers

Provide **certified cancer care**, including:

* Tumor boards
* Psycho-oncology
* Palliative medicine
* Multidisciplinary clinical management

---

#### 3. Organ-Specific Centers

Specialized centers focused on particular cancer types, ensuring **highly specialized treatment expertise**.

---

### 3. Precision Oncology and Molecular Tumor Boards

Modern cancer treatment increasingly relies on **genomic profiling**.

Typical workflow:

1. Collection of **tumor tissue and blood samples**
2. Extraction of **DNA and RNA**
3. Sequencing of **hundreds of cancer-related genes**
4. Identification of **actionable mutations**
5. Selection of **targeted therapies**

Examples of actionable mutations:

* **BRAF V600 mutations** → targeted therapies such as BRAF/MEK inhibitors
* **KIT mutations** → treated with tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs)
* **NRAS mutations** → MEK inhibitors

---

#### Organ-Specific Tumor Board vs Molecular Tumor Board

Traditional tumor boards focus on **clinical decision-making**, involving specialists such as:

* surgeons
* radiologists
* oncologists
* radiation therapists

In contrast, **Molecular Tumor Boards (MTBs)** integrate additional expertise:

* medical genetics
* molecular pathology
* bioinformatics
* genomic medicine specialists

This interdisciplinary approach enables **genome-guided therapy decisions**.

---

### 4. Large-Scale Genomic Programs in Germany

Several national initiatives support precision oncology.

Example:

#### NCT-MASTER Program

A national precision medicine initiative that performs **comprehensive genomic profiling of cancer patients**.

Findings indicate that **broad genomic sequencing significantly improves the identification of actionable biomarkers**.

---

#### German National Genome Strategy

Germany has also initiated a **nationwide genomic medicine program**, aiming to:

* integrate **whole genome sequencing (WGS)** into healthcare
* improve diagnostics for **rare diseases and cancer**
* enable **personalized treatment strategies**

The program is planned for **at least five years**, with significant funding to demonstrate the clinical benefits of genomic medicine.

---

#### Key Takeaways

* Precision oncology relies on **large-scale genomic sequencing and molecular diagnostics**.
* Molecular tumor boards combine **clinical expertise with computational biology**.
* National genome initiatives aim to integrate **genomics into routine medical care**.

The chip integrates:

* **Whispering Gallery Mode (WGM) optical sensors**
* microfluidic channels
* photodiode detection systems

These components enable **high-sensitivity detection of biological signals and biomarkers**.

The system is designed to support **early cancer detection through optical biosensing technologies**.

## Session: AI Practice for Breast Cancer Screening and Workflow

**Speaker:** Sahar Mansour — Faculty of Medicine, Cairo University   
**Type:** Lecture  

---

### 1. Background: Breast Cancer Screening Challenges

Breast cancer remains one of the most common cancers affecting women worldwide. Early detection through **screening mammography** significantly improves survival rates.

However, traditional screening programs face several challenges:

* increasing number of screening examinations
* shortage of trained radiologists
* human fatigue during large screening sessions
* potential **false-negative findings** or missed lesions

Artificial Intelligence has emerged as a powerful tool to assist radiologists in **improving screening accuracy and efficiency**.

---

### 2. AI in Mammography Interpretation

AI systems based on **deep learning and computer vision** can analyze mammography images to detect suspicious patterns such as:

* masses
* architectural distortions
* microcalcifications
* asymmetries

These algorithms produce:

* **heatmaps** highlighting suspicious regions
* **probability scores** indicating cancer likelihood

This allows radiologists to quickly focus on the most relevant image regions during interpretation.

---

### 3. AI-Assisted Double Reading Workflow

Many national screening programs rely on **double reading**, where two radiologists independently review mammograms.

AI can be integrated into this workflow in several ways:

#### 1. AI as an Initial Reader

The AI system first evaluates mammograms and classifies them into:

* likely normal cases
* suspicious cases requiring detailed evaluation

Radiologists then review the AI results and confirm the diagnosis.

---

#### 2. AI as a Second Reader

AI can also act as an **independent second reader**, helping detect abnormalities that a radiologist might miss due to cognitive bias or fatigue.

Studies analyzing **tens of thousands of mammograms** showed that combining AI with a radiologist achieved very high diagnostic accuracy and improved detection rates. 

---

#### 3. AI-Guided Diagnostic Workflow

A modern AI-assisted breast screening workflow may include:

1. Patient mammography acquisition
2. Automated AI analysis of images
3. Heatmap visualization of suspicious lesions
4. Radiologist review and confirmation
5. Follow-up imaging or biopsy when required

This workflow helps streamline the diagnostic process while maintaining clinical oversight.

---

### 4. Clinical Advantages of AI Integration

AI-supported screening systems provide several benefits:

* improved **early detection of small tumors**
* increased diagnostic sensitivity
* reduction in false-negative cases
* improved detection in **dense breast tissue**
* faster workflow in high-volume screening centers

AI also helps reduce the cognitive burden on radiologists by acting as a **decision-support tool rather than replacing clinical expertise**.

---

### Key Takeaways

* Artificial Intelligence enhances the **efficiency and accuracy of breast cancer screening workflows**.
* AI systems can act as **initial readers or second readers** in mammography interpretation.
* Human–AI collaboration improves **early cancer detection and clinical decision-making**.

## Session: Artificial Intelligence Applications in the Diagnosis of Muscle Dystrophies

**Speaker:** Rania Hashem — Faculty of Medicine, Cairo University 
**Type:** Lecture 

---

### 1. Overview of Muscular Dystrophies

Muscular dystrophies are a group of **genetic neuromuscular disorders** characterized by progressive muscle degeneration and weakness.

Examples include:

* Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy (DMD)
* Becker Muscular Dystrophy (BMD)
* Limb-Girdle Muscular Dystrophy (LGMD)

Early and accurate diagnosis is critical for:

* clinical management
* genetic counseling
* therapeutic interventions

However, diagnosis can be complex because symptoms often overlap with other neuromuscular diseases.

---

### 2. Role of Artificial Intelligence in Neuromuscular Diagnosis

Artificial intelligence technologies are increasingly used to assist clinicians in analyzing large biomedical datasets related to muscular diseases.

AI applications include:

* analysis of **medical imaging**
* interpretation of **genomic sequencing data**
* automated detection of **muscle degeneration patterns**

Machine learning algorithms can detect subtle patterns that may be difficult for human observers to identify.

---

### 3. AI in Medical Imaging for Muscle Disorders

AI tools can analyze imaging modalities such as:

* **Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)**
* **Ultrasound imaging**
* histopathological muscle biopsy images

These systems help:

* quantify muscle degeneration
* detect fatty infiltration in muscle tissues
* classify disease subtypes

Deep learning models can also differentiate between different **types of muscular dystrophies based on imaging patterns**.

---

### 4. AI in Genomic and Molecular Diagnosis

Genetic testing plays a central role in diagnosing muscular dystrophies.

AI can assist by:

* analyzing large genomic datasets
* identifying pathogenic mutations
* predicting disease severity
* supporting genotype–phenotype correlation analysis

This accelerates the identification of **disease-causing genetic variants**.

---

### 5. Clinical Decision Support

AI-driven diagnostic systems may combine multiple data sources:

* genetic data
* clinical symptoms
* imaging findings
* laboratory results

This integrated approach improves diagnostic accuracy and enables **personalized treatment planning**.

---

### Key Takeaways

* Artificial Intelligence supports **early and accurate diagnosis of neuromuscular disorders**.
* AI can integrate **imaging, genomic, and clinical data** to improve diagnostic precision.
* Computational tools are increasingly important in **rare disease diagnostics and precision medicine**.

## Workshop: Single-Cell Transcriptomics — Insights into Cell Diversity and Tumor Heterogeneity[Day 1]

**Instructors**

* **Prof. Dr. Mohamed Hamed** — GUC & Rostock University Medical Center   
* **Dr. Asmaa Mohamed Reda** — Zewail City University / Benha University / King Abdulaziz University 

**Type:** Lecture & Practical

---

### Part 1 — Lecture: Introduction to Single-Cell Transcriptomics

#### Core Concepts

Single-cell RNA sequencing (**scRNA-seq**) enables the analysis of **gene expression at the level of individual cells**, allowing researchers to study cellular heterogeneity within tissues.

This approach is particularly important in cancer research because tumors often contain **multiple cell populations with distinct molecular profiles**.

Topics covered included:

* structure of **single-cell gene expression matrices**
* preprocessing and normalization of scRNA-seq data
* quality control and filtering of cells
* dimensionality reduction techniques such as **PCA and UMAP**
* clustering of cells based on transcriptomic profiles
* annotation of cell types
* identification of **malignant vs non-malignant cell populations**
* trajectory analysis for studying **tumor evolution**

---

### Part 2 — Practical: Interactive Exploration of scRNA-seq Data

Participants explored **single-cell datasets using interactive visualization tools**, including:

* **CELLxGENE**
* **bxGenomics**

These tools enable:

* exploration of cell clusters
* visualization of gene expression patterns
* comparison of expression levels across cell populations
* integration of metadata such as sample origin and disease state

Interactive visualization allows researchers to **identify biological patterns and generate hypotheses** from complex single-cell datasets.

---

#### Key Takeaways

* Single-cell transcriptomics provides unprecedented insights into **tumor heterogeneity**.
* Computational tools are essential for analyzing **high-dimensional single-cell datasets**.
* Visualization platforms allow researchers to **interactively explore complex transcriptomic landscapes**.
