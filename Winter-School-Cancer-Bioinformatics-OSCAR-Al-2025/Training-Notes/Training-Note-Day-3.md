## Session: Seeing Cancer Differently — How VR, Digital Twins and AI Help Patients and Researchers

**Speaker:** Dr. Rana Youmess — German International University (GIU), Egypt  
**Type:** Lecture 

#### Key Concepts

* **Virtual Reality (VR)** technologies enable immersive visualization of biological systems, allowing researchers and clinicians to explore complex medical data in interactive environments.
* **Digital twins** represent computational models of biological systems or individual patients that replicate physiological processes using real-world data.
* Combining VR with **AI-driven modeling** allows clinicians to simulate disease progression, treatment responses, and surgical procedures.
* These technologies can support **patient education**, helping patients understand their diagnosis and treatment options through visual simulations.
* Digital twins may assist in **personalized medicine**, where patient-specific data are used to model individual treatment responses.

#### Key Takeaways

* Immersive technologies such as VR can transform how researchers and clinicians **visualize and interpret medical data**.
* Digital twins and AI models can improve **personalized treatment planning and medical decision support**.

## Session: Green Intelligence — Designing LLMs for a Sustainable Future

**Speaker:** Dr. Aya Salama — German University in Cairo (GUC), Egypt   
**Type:** Lecture 

#### Key Concepts

* Modern **large language models (LLMs)** require substantial computational resources for training and deployment.
* The concept of **Green AI** emphasizes designing machine learning systems that minimize environmental impact while maintaining performance.
* Strategies for sustainable AI include:

  * improving model efficiency
  * reducing computational redundancy
  * optimizing hardware utilization
  * using energy-efficient training techniques
* Efficient model architectures can reduce **energy consumption and carbon footprint** of AI systems.
* Sustainable AI is increasingly important as machine learning models grow in size and complexity.

#### Key Takeaways

* Sustainable AI development requires balancing **model performance with computational efficiency**.
* Green AI principles are essential for the **long-term scalability of AI technologies**.

## Session: AI-Driven 6G Communication Sensing for Healthcare — Anomaly Detection, Behavioral Monitoring, and Fall Risk Assessment

**Speaker:** Prof. Dr. Mahmoud Shawky — German International University (GIU), Egypt   
**Type:** Lecture 

#### 1. Modern Healthcare Challenges

Healthcare systems worldwide face several challenges:

* Increasing demand for **medical services**
* Limited hospital resources
* Need for **continuous monitoring of vulnerable populations**

These challenges motivate the development of **remote healthcare monitoring systems**.

---

#### 2. Remote Healthcare Monitoring Systems

A modern remote monitoring framework often integrates:

* **Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS)**
* **Internet of Things (IoT) devices**
* **Artificial Intelligence algorithms**
* Advanced communication networks

Monitoring technologies may include:

* **Vision-based systems**
* **Wearable sensors**
* **Radio-frequency sensing systems**

Each approach provides different types of physiological or behavioral information.

---

#### 3. AI-Based Behavioral Monitoring

AI algorithms can analyze continuous sensor data to detect:

* abnormal behavioral patterns
* changes in daily activity levels
* potential medical emergencies

These systems can support **fall detection**, which is particularly important for elderly patients.

---

#### 4. Radio-Frequency (RF) Sensing

RF sensing technologies allow **contactless monitoring** of human movement and physiological signals.

Potential advantages include:

* non-invasive monitoring
* continuous data collection
* minimal user interaction

Applications include:

* activity recognition
* fall detection
* health monitoring in smart environments

---

#### 5. Future Research Directions

Future work includes:

* improving system accuracy across **multiple individuals**
* testing systems in **different real-world environments**
* integrating clinical expertise into system design
* collaboration with healthcare providers and industry partners

---

#### Key Takeaways

* AI-powered sensing technologies enable **contactless and continuous health monitoring**.
* Remote monitoring systems may reduce healthcare burden while improving **patient safety and early intervention**.

## Session: Analytical Techniques for Human Metabolomics    

**Speaker:** Prof. Dr. Rasha Hanafy — German University in Cairo (GUC), Egypt    
**Type:** Lecture 

#### Key Concepts

* **Metabolomics** studies small molecules (metabolites) present in biological systems.
* These metabolites represent the **final products of cellular processes**, providing insights into physiological and pathological states.
* Analytical techniques commonly used in metabolomics include:

  * **Mass Spectrometry (MS)**
  * **Liquid Chromatography (LC-MS)**
  * **Gas Chromatography (GC-MS)**
  * **Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR) spectroscopy**
* Metabolomics data help identify **biomarkers associated with diseases**, including cancer and metabolic disorders.
* Integrating metabolomics with **genomics and transcriptomics** supports comprehensive multi-omics analysis.

#### Key Takeaways

* Metabolomics provides critical insights into **cellular metabolism and disease mechanisms**.
* Analytical technologies enable detection of **metabolic biomarkers for diagnostic and research applications**.

## Workshop: Single-Cell Transcriptomics — Insights into Cell Diversity and Tumor Heterogeneity [Day 3]

**Type:** Hands-on Practical Session  

### Project Overview

On the **third day of the workshop**, participants undertook **mini-projects** and presented their results. Each project differed between students. During these mini-projects, participants applied computational methods to analyze **single-cell RNA sequencing (scRNA-seq) datasets**.

For my project, I focused on:

Building a **reproducible R Markdown analysis pipeline** to study **glioblastoma single-cell transcriptomic data**.
**Student:** Mohamed H. Hussein
**Tools/Packages used:** Seurat, SeuratDisk, tidyverse, SCTransform, UMAP, PCA

---

### Dataset

* **Cancer Type:** Adult Glioblastoma (GBM)
* **Technology:** 10x Genomics scRNA-seq
* **Cell Populations:**

  * CD45⁻ glioblastoma tumor cells
  * CD45⁺ immune cells

---

### Analytical Workflow

#### 1. Data Loading & Preprocessing

* Importing count matrices and cell metadata
* Creating **Seurat objects** for downstream analysis

#### 2. Quality Control

* Filtering cells based on gene counts and mitochondrial gene expression
* Removing low-quality or dying cells

#### 3. Batch Correction & Dataset Integration

* Integrating multiple patient datasets
* Reducing batch effects using **SCTransform normalization**

#### 4. Dimensionality Reduction & Clustering

* **Principal Component Analysis (PCA)**
* **UMAP visualization**
* Clustering based on transcriptional similarity

#### 5. Marker Gene Identification

* Detecting cluster-specific genes
* Identifying **tumor-related** and **immune-related markers**

#### 6. Tumor Heterogeneity Analysis

* Comparing gene expression across patients
* Exploring variation in tumor microenvironment composition

---

### Key Takeaways

* Gained **practical experience** building reproducible scRNA-seq analysis pipelines using **R and Seurat**.
* Understood the importance of **quality control** and **batch correction** in single-cell datasets.
* Developed the ability to identify **tumor-associated cellular populations** and **transcriptomic markers**.
