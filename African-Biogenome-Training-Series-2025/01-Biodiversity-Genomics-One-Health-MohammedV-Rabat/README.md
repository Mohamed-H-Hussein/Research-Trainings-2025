## 📘 Overview

The workshop introduced key concepts at the intersection of:

* Genomics and biodiversity research
* One Health frameworks
* Bioinformatics technologies
* Ethical and policy governance in genomics
* Genome sequencing platforms and workflows

Through expert lectures and scientific discussions, participants explored how genomics supports **health security, conservation biology, sustainable agriculture, and regional scientific capacity building across Africa**.

* Understanding **genomics-driven strategies** supporting health, biodiversity conservation, and sustainable agriculture
* Integration of **biodiversity genomics** with ecological, environmental, and public health systems
* Applications of **bioinformatics approaches** for monitoring, prediction, and ecosystem management
* Awareness of **ethical, legal, and governance frameworks** in biodiversity genomics projects
* Conceptual understanding of **high-throughput sequencing technologies** (Illumina, MGI, Megaflex)
* Principles of **genome annotation** and **CRISPR/Cas9 experimental design** in plant and animal biology
* Understanding reproducible **bioinformatics workflows** using Nextflow in OMICS and RNA-seq contexts
* Familiarity with **genomic barcoding and biosurveillance** strategies for species identification
* Appreciation of **AI and genomic language models** in functional genomics research
* Ability to interpret interdisciplinary genomic applications across conservation, agriculture, and One Health frameworks
