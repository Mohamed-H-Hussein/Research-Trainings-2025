# 🧬 African BioGenome Project (AfricaBP) — Biodiversity Genomics & One Health Workshop 
## Day 2 — Training Notes and Scientific Learning Outcomes

Day 2 expanded the workshop discussions toward emerging genome technologies, and applied bioinformatics practices supporting biodiversity genomics research across Africa. Emphasis was placed on sequencing infrastructures, policy considerations, reproducible workflows, genome annotation, and computational approaches for biodiversity monitoring and health-related research.

Thus, this document presents my personal study notes and learning outcomes from the Day 2 sessions, including oral presentations and practical workshop introductions.

## **Training Summary & Skills Acquired — Day 2**

### **Training Summary**

Day 2 emphasized governance, genome technologies, practical bioinformatics, and hands-on exercises for biodiversity, agriculture, and public health applications.

Key learnings:

* Integrating **ethical and legal frameworks** with genomic research and biodiversity governance
* Applying **advanced sequencing platforms** (Illumina, MGI, Megaflex) in research and agriculture
* Conducting **practical bioinformatics workflows** with Nextflow and computational pipelines
* Implementing **genome annotation and CRISPR/Cas9 experiments** in plant and animal biology
* Translating genomic insights into **conservation, public health, and food security strategies**

---

* Ethical governance and policy frameworks for genomic research
* Large-scale sequencing platforms and their comparative applications
* End-to-end bioinformatics workflow design and reproducibility

* Practical applications of Nextflow in biodiversity and OMICS projects
