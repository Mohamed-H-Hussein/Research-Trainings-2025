# 🧬 African BioGenome Project (AfricaBP) — Biodiversity Genomics & One Health Workshop
## Day 1 — Training Notes and Scientific Learning Outcomes

The workshop provided a multidisciplinary platform bringing together genomics, bioinformatics, biodiversity science, and health-oriented research perspectives. Day 1 sessions focused on genomics-driven One Health strategies, highlighting large-scale genome initiatives, bioinformatics technologies, and applied genomics addressing ecological and infectious disease challenges.

Thus, this document summarizes my personal study notes and key learning outcomes from the Day 1 scientific sessions and expert discussions.

## **Training Summary & Skills Acquired — Day**

### **Training Summary**

Day 1 of the **African BioGenome Project (AfricaBP) — Biodiversity Genomics & One Health Workshop** provided a platform to understand the convergence of genomics, bioinformatics, and biodiversity within One Health frameworks in Africa.

Key learnings:

* Support **One Health strategies**, integrating human, animal, and environmental health
* Enable **genomic surveillance** for pathogens and ecological threats
* Facilitate **large-scale biodiversity genome projects** for conservation and ecosystem management
* Apply **bioinformatics technologies** in agriculture, aquaculture, and microbial ecosystems
* Translate genomics insights into **policy, preparedness, and sustainable development strategies**

* Understanding of **genomic language models (gLMs)** and AI in genomics
* Translational genomics for **conservation, food security, and ecosystem monitoring**
