## 📘 Overview

The **Intensive Practical Genome Sequencing Course** introduced participants to **genome sequencing workflows, metadata management, genome assembly, annotation, and reproducible bioinformatics pipelines**. The program combined **lectures, hands-on wet-lab training, computational tutorials, and workflow management exercises**, providing participants with:

* Strategic planning of genome sequencing experiments
* Hands-on **DNA extraction, library preparation, and sequencing QC**
* Introduction to **long-read sequencing technologies** (Oxford Nanopore)
* Genome assembly and evaluation (graph-based and k-mer–based approaches)
* Genome annotation using Ensembl and multi-evidence gene prediction frameworks
* Reproducible **bioinformatics pipelines** using Conda, Docker, and Nextflow
* Metadata management and data submission to **ENA repositories**

> Detailed daily session notes are available in the [Event-Notes folder](./Event-Notes).

## 🎯 Relevance to My Field

As an MSc candidate in **Biochemistry & Molecular Biology**, specializing in **Molecular Cancer Biology** and **Bioinformatics**, this intensive genomics course provided:

* **Hands-on experience** with wet-lab and computational genome sequencing workflows.
* Exposure to **long-read sequencing technologies** and library preparation methods.
* Practical skills in **genome assembly, annotation, and QC** pipelines.
* Training in **reproducible bioinformatics workflows** using Conda, Docker, and Nextflow.
* Experience in **metadata management and submission to ENA**, enhancing rigor and reproducibility.
* Development of **critical reasoning for interpreting assembly quality, functional annotation, and comparative genomics results**.

This training **strengthened my ability to integrate experimental and computational approaches**, directly relevant to **molecular cancer biology research** and future **bioinformatics-driven projects**.
