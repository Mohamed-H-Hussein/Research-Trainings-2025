## 🏫 Training Overview & Event Details

### 1. Program Details

* **Institution:** University of Tsukuba
* **Program Title:** Summer Research Program 2025 Online (SRP-2025)
* **Host Program:** Master’s Program in Medical Sciences
* **Academic Field:** Medicine, Biomedical & Life Sciences
* **Application Period:** August 1 – August 20, 2025
* **Program Period:** September 10 – September 19, 2025
* **Duration:** 2 weeks
* **Language of Instruction:** English
* **Delivery Format:** Fully online, combining on-demand lectures, live sessions, interactive Q&A, and mini-tests
* **Delivery Mode:**
  * Instructor-led video lectures (on-demand)
  * Live Zoom sessions 
  * Structured discussions and interactive Q&A
* **Official Program Announcement:**
  [Program Announcement Link](https://int-office.md.tsukuba.ac.jp/471/)

---

### 2. Live Support & Interactive Learning

The program combined structured digital learning with real-time academic engagement:

**Recorded Lectures**

* Pre-recorded PI sessions accessible via LMS
* Each recorded lecture was followed by a **dedicated live zoom Q&A session**
* Enabled clarification of research concepts, methodologies, and experimental approaches

**Live Lectures**
* Delivered in real time via Zoom
* Each session concluded with a **Q&A segment**
* Allowed direct interaction with faculty members
 
**Interactive  Q&A sessions:**
  * Structured sessions available for both recorded and live lectures
  * Focused on research methodology, hypothesis evaluation, problem-solving, and practical applications


---

### 3. Mini-Tests & Completion Criteria

Each Principal Investigator (PI) lecture included an assessment component:

* **Total Mini-Tests:** 13 (one per PI lecture)
* **Access Method:** LMS-based, password provided during lecture
* **Mini-Tests Requirement:** Full score required to pass each mini-test
* **Program Completion:**
  Participants were required to attend at least four PI sessions (live or on-demand) and pass the associated mini-tests to qualify for certification.

## 📘 Program Overview & Scientific Scope

The **SRP-2025 Online** provided structured exposure to cutting-edge biomedical research areas at the University of Tsukuba through lectures delivered by active Principal Investigators.


**Core Scientific Domains Covered**

* **Molecular & Genome Biology**
* **Infection & Immune Sciences**
* **Biomedical & Translational Medicine**
* **Structural & Developmental Biology**
* **Chemical & Computational Drug Discovery**

Through these domains, participants:

* Engaged with contemporary research questions and experimental strategies
* Explored translational applications from bench to clinic
* Developed logical thinking and problem-solving skills
* Practiced identifying scientific problems and evaluating hypotheses
* Enhanced global academic perspective through interaction with internationally active researchers

The program was designed for:
* Senior undergraduate students
* Master’s students in life sciences or related fields

## 🎯 Academic Relevance

This program strengthened my skills as an MSc candidate in **Biochemistry & Molecular Biology (Molecular Cancer Biology & Bioinformatics)** by:

* Deepening understanding of **post-transcriptional gene regulation, RNA stability, translation, and decay**, relevant to cancer biology mechanisms
* Providing hands-on exposure to **single-cell RNA sequencing, multi-omics, and bioinformatic integration**, enhancing computational analysis skills
* Developing expertise in **viral and bacterial host-pathogen interactions**, with implications for cancer immunology and tumor microenvironment studies
* Applying **genetically modified mouse models** to investigate molecular pathways and disease mechanisms
* Integrating experimental and computational approaches across **molecular, cellular, and systems biology**, fostering mechanistic insights for translational research
* Practicing **rigorous experimental design, reproducibility, and ethical research conduct**, critical for independent Research investigations

## 🛠️ Skills Developed

* **Molecular & RNA Biology for Cancer Research**

  * Analysis of **mRNA structure, translation, and miRNA regulation** in cellular heterogeneity
  * Understanding **RNA-binding proteins, decay complexes (Ccr4-Not)**, and post-transcriptional control in cancer-relevant contexts

* **Single-Cell & Multi-Omics Integration**

  * Processing and analyzing **scRNA-seq datasets** for tumor heterogeneity
  * Integration of **transcriptomic, epigenomic, and proteomic data** to study gene regulatory networks
  * Data reproducibility and management in **bioinformatics pipelines**

* **Cancer & Immunology Insights**

  * Modeling tumor biology using **engineered mouse models** and **primary human samples**
  * Evaluating **immune evasion mechanisms, tumor microenvironment dynamics, and clonal hematopoiesis**
  * Linking **experimental observations to therapeutic strategies**

* **Experimental Design & Translational Competence**

  * Planning experiments across **molecular, cellular, and animal systems**
  * Executing **library preparation, QC, imaging, electrophysiology**, and multi-omics data collection
  * Developing **analytical pipelines for reproducible research**

* **Computational Biology & Drug Discovery Readiness**

  * Application of **ligand/structure-based drug design, molecular docking, and AI-driven modeling**
  * Integration of **in silico predictions with experimental validation**
  * Preparing for **computational cancer biology and systems medicine projects**

* **Broader Scientific Competencies**

  * Interdisciplinary problem-solving bridging **molecular, cellular, and computational biology**
  * Translating research findings to **mechanistic insights and translational relevance**
